Should First World Countries intervene with Third World Countries?

The definition of a first-world country is “a term developed during the Cold War in the 1950s, originally referred to a country that wasn’t
aligned with the United States and other western nations in opposition to what was then the Soviet Union and its allies. Since the
collapse of the Soviet Union in 1991, the term's meaning has largely evolved. And the definition of a third-world country is “a phrase
that has been used historically to describe a class of economically developing nations... Today the preferred terminology is a developing
nation, an underdeveloped country, or a low and middle-income country”.

Since the Cold War, there have been debates on whether or not
First World Countries should provide insight or dictate the decisions of
third world countries. This includes interventions, war, and invasion. In
our opinion, we think third-world countries should be left alone.
One such example of first-world country intervention is the
Vietnam War. The US was afraid of the spread of communism in
South Vietnam and the rest of Asia. So they first sent
money, supplies, and military advisers to South Vietnam. Then,
President Johnson decided to deploy troops in March 1965. With this
came ten years of war against the North Vietnamese and a
war that would define a decade and cause major changes. It
also cost the US 58,220 deaths, 521 Australian deaths, 37 New Zealander
deaths, 5,099 South Korean deaths, 200,000-250,000 South Vietnamese
deaths, 1.1 Million North Vietnamese deaths, and 2,000,000
million civilian deaths.




To this day America's intervention in Vietnam is still causing people to suffer. During the war, America spread a chemical called
Agent Orange. This chemical kills plants and foliage to stop the Vietcong from hiding their weapons, supplies and themselves.
This chemical didn’t just kill foliage though, it killed animals and humans. Agent Orange didn’t just go away after a while, it's
lingering just to this day. In Vietnam today, the chemical has caused birth defects, poisoning of crops and animals and
therefore disrupting the food chain and have deformed Vietnam’s land. In fact, some Veterans from the war even died a
few years later from the chemicals they inhaled. This is all from America’s intervention to try and help third world countries.

As stated before, we think Third World Countries should be left alone. We think of this because of the outcomes of The First
World Countries intervening, providing insight, or dictating. This could cause small and large-scale conflicts. Undeveloped
countries could be taken advantage of, exploited, worsen the situation or worsen the population and put it into darkness. When
countries become or are Third World Countries it is because they have large numbers of people in poverty, they’re economically
poor, non-industrialized, have a poor health care system, and a low Human Development Index. So in turn, we think that First
World Countries should not provide insight or dictate the decisions of Third World Countries.
